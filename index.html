I am a Ph.D. candidate at KAUST Computational Imaging Group, working with Prof. Wolfgang Heidrich. My research focuses on end-to-end imaging simulation and differentiable optical design. I aim to bridge the sim2real gap and explore the next generation imaging/sensing systems.

My representative work published in Nature Communications enables automated lens design with differentiable optimization. I maintain an open-source camera simulator DeepLens for end-to-end differentiable simulation and optimization of imaging systems, including optics, sensor, and computer vision.
